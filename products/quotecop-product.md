# SECTION 2: AI QUOTING PROMPT SYSTEM

## The Master Quote Prompt

Copy this entire prompt and paste it into ChatGPT or Claude. Fill in the [BRACKETS].

---

**PROMPT:**

```
You are an expert freelance business consultant helping me write a professional project quote. 

Here is the project:
- Client name/business: [NAME]
- Project type: [e.g. website redesign, logo design, social media management]
- Project details: [describe what they asked for]
- Timeline they mentioned: [e.g. "need it in 3 weeks" or "no rush"]
- Their budget (if mentioned): [e.g. "$2,000" or "they didn't say"]
- My experience level: [beginner / intermediate / expert]
- My location: [city, country]

Please create:
1. A professional quote breakdown with line items and pricing
2. A clear scope of work (what's included)
3. What's NOT included (to prevent scope creep)
4. Payment terms recommendation
5. Timeline breakdown
6. A professional email I can send to the client with this quote attached

Use confident, professional language. Price fairly but don't undercharge — I am a skilled professional.
```

---

## Quick Quote Prompt (For Fast Turnaround)

```
I'm a freelance [YOUR SKILL] and a client just asked me to [DESCRIBE PROJECT]. They want it done in [TIMELINE]. Give me:
1. A fair price range (low / mid / high)
2. 3 bullet points of what's included
3. One sentence about what's NOT included
4. A one-paragraph professional response I can send them right now
```

# SECTION 4: SCOPE CREEP SCRIPTS

## What is scope creep?
When a client asks for "just one more thing" that wasn't in the original agreement. It's the #1 way freelancers lose money. Use these scripts word for word.

---

## Script 1 — "Can you just add one more thing?"

> "Happy to add that! That falls outside what we originally agreed on, so I'll put together a quick change order. It'll be an additional $[AMOUNT] and add [X] days to the timeline. Want me to send that over?"

---

## Script 2 — "I thought that was included"

> "I completely understand — let me pull up the original scope so we're on the same page. [Reference the contract/email.] That specific item wasn't part of what we agreed on, but I'd love to help. I can add it for $[AMOUNT]. Shall I send a quick invoice for that?"

---

## Script 3 — "It's just a small change"

> "I hear you — it seems small but it actually requires [X hours / rebuilding Y / reworking Z]. To keep things fair on both sides I'll need to charge $[AMOUNT] for that. Want me to go ahead?"

---

## Script 4 — Client keeps changing direction

> "I want to make sure we get this exactly right for you. We've now gone through [X] rounds of revisions, which is beyond the [X] included in our agreement. Going forward, additional revisions are $[AMOUNT] each. I'll keep a running tally and invoice at the end. Sound good?"

---

## Script 5 — "Can we add this for free since we're working together?"

> "I really value our relationship and want to keep working together long-term — which is exactly why I need to keep my business sustainable. I can offer you a discounted rate of $[AMOUNT] for this addition instead of my normal $[AMOUNT]. Fair?"

# SECTION 5: RATE CALCULATOR

## How to calculate your hourly rate

**Formula:**
> Target Annual Income ÷ Billable Hours Per Year = Minimum Hourly Rate

**Then multiply by 1.5–2x** to account for: taxes, unpaid admin time, slow months, software costs, and profit margin.

**Billable hours per year** (realistic):
- Full-time freelancer: ~1,000 hrs/yr (not 2,080 — you won't be booked solid)
- Part-time freelancer: ~500 hrs/yr

---

## Rate Table by Income Target

| Target Income | Billable Hrs/Yr | Min Rate | Recommended Rate |
|---------------|-----------------|----------|------------------|
| $30,000 | 1,000 | $30/hr | $45–$60/hr |
| $50,000 | 1,000 | $50/hr | $75–$100/hr |
| $75,000 | 1,000 | $75/hr | $110–$150/hr |
| $100,000 | 1,000 | $100/hr | $150–$200/hr |
| $150,000 | 1,000 | $150/hr | $225–$300/hr |
| $200,000 | 1,000 | $200/hr | $300–$400/hr |

---

## Project Rate Formula

> Estimated Hours × Hourly Rate × 1.25 (buffer) = Project Price

**Example:** Logo design, estimate 8 hrs, rate $100/hr
8 × $100 × 1.25 = **$1,000**

Always add the 1.25 buffer. Projects always take longer than you think.

---

## Value-Based Pricing (Charge More)

Instead of hours, charge based on the value you create for the client.

**Formula:**
> What is this project worth to the client? × 10–20% = Your Fee

**Example:** You're building a landing page for a client who expects $50,000 in sales from it.
$50,000 × 15% = **$7,500 fee** (regardless of hours)

Use this when:
- Client has clear revenue goals
- You have a track record of results
- The project has measurable ROI

# SECTION 6: RED FLAG CLIENT DETECTOR

## 10 Warning Signs — And What To Do

**Red Flag 1: "This should be quick/easy for you"**
Translation: They'll lowball you and expect it fast.
Action: Respond with a full scope and price. Don't adjust because they said it's easy.

**Red Flag 2: "We don't have a big budget but there's potential for more work"**
Translation: They want a discount now and the "more work" rarely materializes.
Action: Charge your full rate. Offer a smaller scope to fit their budget, not a lower rate.

**Red Flag 3: "Can you do a test project first?"**
Translation: Free work dressed up as an audition.
Action: "I don't do spec work, but I'm happy to show you my portfolio. If you'd like a small paid pilot project, I can do [X] for $[AMOUNT]."

**Red Flag 4: They can't explain what they want**
Translation: Endless revisions and a moving target.
Action: Run a paid discovery session ($150–$500) before quoting. Scope the project properly first.

**Red Flag 5: "The last freelancer couldn't get it right"**
Translation: They may be the problem, not the freelancer.
Action: Ask specifically what went wrong and why. Get more context before agreeing.

**Red Flag 6: They want to skip the contract**
Translation: No accountability when things go sideways.
Action: Non-negotiable. Always have a contract. Offer a simple 1-page agreement if they're intimidated by long contracts.

**Red Flag 7: They haggle aggressively on price before seeing your work**
Translation: Price is their only priority — not quality.
Action: Offer a stripped-down package or walk away. Clients who haggle before hiring haggle on every invoice.

**Red Flag 8: They want full rights/ownership for a low price**
Translation: Huge value extraction for minimum pay.
Action: Charge a licensing fee. Full buyout of rights = 2–3x your base rate.

**Red Flag 9: Unrealistic deadlines**
Translation: They'll be angry when you miss a deadline they forced on you.
Action: Be honest about timeline. Charge a rush fee (25–50% premium) if they insist.

**Red Flag 10: Slow to respond, then expects instant turnaround**
Translation: You'll wait weeks for feedback, then scramble.
Action: Add a response time clause to your contract: "Client has 48 hours to review and respond. Delays caused by client will extend the timeline accordingly."

# SECTION 8: CONTRACT CLAUSES (COPY-PASTE)

Add these to any contract or agreement to protect yourself.

---

**Scope Clause:**
> "This agreement covers only the deliverables listed above. Any additional work requested beyond this scope will be quoted separately and requires written approval before work begins."

**Revision Clause:**
> "This project includes [X] rounds of revisions. A revision round is defined as one consolidated set of feedback. Additional revision rounds are billed at $[AMOUNT] per round."

**Payment Clause:**
> "A non-refundable deposit of 50% is due before work begins. The remaining 50% is due upon project completion, before final files are delivered."

**Late Payment Clause:**
> "Invoices unpaid after 14 days will incur a 1.5% monthly late fee. Work will be paused on all active projects until outstanding invoices are settled."

**Kill Fee Clause:**
> "If this project is cancelled by the client after work has begun, the deposit is non-refundable. If more than 50% of the work is complete at time of cancellation, the full project fee is owed."

**Ownership Clause:**
> "Full ownership of final deliverables transfers to the client upon receipt of final payment. All work product remains the property of [YOUR NAME] until paid in full."

**Timeline Clause:**
> "Project timeline begins upon receipt of deposit and all required client materials. Delays caused by late client feedback will extend the delivery date accordingly."

**Confidentiality Clause:**
> "Both parties agree to keep confidential any proprietary information shared during this project. This does not prevent [YOUR NAME] from displaying the completed work in their portfolio unless otherwise agreed in writing."
